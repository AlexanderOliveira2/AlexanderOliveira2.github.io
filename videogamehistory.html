History of Video Games

Video games are digital games that can be played on a computer device (e.g., tablets, smartphones, laptops, consoles, etc.) Originally designed simply for entertainment, video games have grown into their own industry and field of learning, generating billions of dollars each year. The North American Video Game Crash (1983)

In the time period of 1983, the gaming industry was barely a fraction of what it is now, making it considered as a niche genre, or a fad. Eventually, an event called the “North American Video Game Crash would occur, which almost spelled the end for video gaming entirely. One of the main reasons for the Crash was major oversaturation of the market. At the time, there were dozens of consoles to choose from and many of their own games made for them. Some of these consoles were

  • The Atari 2600,
  • The Magnavox Odyssey,
  • And the ColecoVision.
